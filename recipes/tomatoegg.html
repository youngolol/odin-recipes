Chinese Tomato Egg Stir-Fry

Description:

Right at the height of COVID lockdowns, I was living out of a small town in Norther California, just beyond San Francisco, with a Chinese family. I was working on a contract, and found a spare room within their home. Although I was there a short time, and despite there being a bit of a language barrier between us, I learned quite a lot about the family through cooking! One of the first things they taught me to make was tomato egg; I think what I was really drawn to beyond the great flavor combination, was just the simplicity of the dish to make on my "lazy" nights.

Ingredients (to feed 1-2)

  • 3 small tomatoes (I find that roma's are nice for this dish)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 green onion (finely sliced)
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp shaoxing wine
  • 3-4 tbp neutral oil
  • 1-2 tsp white sugar
  • salt to taste

Steps:

  1. Cut the tomatoes into small wedges (usually sixths)
  2. Mince the garlic
  3. Whisk the eggs, with the shaoxing wine, until homogenous and yolks blended well into the whites
  4. Preheat pan over medium heat then add 1/2 neutral oil
  5. Add the eggs, and scramble for approximately 1-1.5min (eggs should be runny between completely cooked and liquid)
  6. Remove eggs from pan into another plate or bowl
  7. Add the other half of the oil and then the garlic to cook until aromatic (not burnt)
  8. Add tomatoes and cook for 3min or until medium soft
  9. Add eggs and white pepper and mix together
  10. Add salt to taste and then green onions as garnish
  11. Serve with rice and enjoy!
