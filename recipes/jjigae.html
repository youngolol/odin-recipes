Kimchi-Jjigae (also known as kimchi stew)

Description:

One of my favorite dishes from my childhood that I think reminds me of my mom the most.

This is for you, umma!

Ingredients:

  • 1/3 pound pork belly
  • 1/2 square of tofu cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1 pound kimchi (cut into bite sized pieces)
  • 1/3 cup kimchi juice
  • 1 medium, yellow onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 teaspoons korean hot pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1 tablespoon korean red pepper paste (gochujang)
  • 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
  • 3 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 cups of anchovy stock (revealed later in recipe)
  • 2 green onion cut into 1/4 inch diagonal slices
  • 10 large, dried anchovies (guts removed)
  • 1/2 cup daikon (quartered)
  • 200g dried kelp
  • 5 cups water
  • salt to taste

Steps:

  1. To make the stock, combine: anchovies, kelp, daikon, and water in a pot to boil for 25min on medium-high heat then strain the stock from the solid ingredients
  2. Place kimchi with its juice, pork belly, and sliced yellow and half green onion into a pot
  3. Combine with sugar, pepper flakes, and stock made earlier
  4. Bring to boil, add the pepper paste, then cover pot and cook for 10-12min over medium-heat
  5. Add sesame oil and tofu, then cover and cook for another 15min
  6. Add remaining green onions then salt to taste
  7. Serve with rice!
